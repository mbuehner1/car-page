1. The Ford Mustang

The Ford Mustang is, if not the, most iconic American car.
It first entered production in 1967 and has continued to be produce by the Ford Motor Company.
Its name actually comes from the P-51 Mustang, a WWII era fighter plane

2. The Chevrolet Camaro

The Chevrolet Camaro is the second most iconic American car.
It was also entered into production in 1967, to rival the Ford Mustang.
It is still produced today, although the Ford Mustang has sold more units by millions.

3. The Dodge Challenger

The Dodge Challenger is another iconic American car. It was first produced in 1974.
It has changed many hands for its manufacturers', even being manufactured by Mistubishi Motors in 1978-83.

4. The Dodge Viper

The Dodge Viper was first produced in 1991 as a sports car.
It also changed many manufacturers, do to it being owned by Dodge.
Development was almost shut down in 2010, but a new concept design was released and more demand for the car quicky spread.

5. The Porsche 718

The Porsche 718 is one of the most affordable Porsches' on the market.
It is very new, as it was first released in 2017.
So far, 25,000 have been released to customers in just 2 years.
